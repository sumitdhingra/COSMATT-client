ABOUT THE COURSE

DESCRIPTION

COSMATT Financial Accounting is a digital first Courseware and a Set of Problems available as a bundle or separately. The courseware is an online study material which includes interactive spreadsheet-based in-line exercises, real-time feedback and graded assignments. The Problem Sets are a set of individualized spreadsheet-based exercises that can be used for homework assignments or for assessments. All problems are academically-vetted and linked to common learning objectives in many popular introductory financial accounting texts. The questions cover the fundamentals of financial accounting including recording transactions, the accounting cycle, financial statements and investment analysis.

COSMATT Financial Accounting Course

Introduction to Financial Accounting teaches the fundamentals of financial accounting from the ground up. Students learn to prepare and analyze financial statements using real-world case studies. Learners discover how to use their understanding of accounting to drive insights into business performance.

What Students Will Learn


  • The accounting equations and how to classify business transactions into accounts
  • The double-entry accounting system and how to journalize transactions
  • To Post transactions from the journal to the ledger accounts
  • The steps in the accounting cycle
  • The financial statements and how they are developed and interrelated
  • To make strategic investment decisions by forecasting cash flows and valuing projects and ventures
All the course content is developed in collaboration with contributing author - Christine Jonick, Professor of Accounting, University of North Georgia, Gainesville and author of “Principles of Financial Accounting”

John Durrant

RETIRED SERVO ENGINEER

I qualified as an Electrical Engineer, with an electronic bias. I spent a few years in the Thyristor drive development laboratory of a major UK electrical equipment manufacturer, where I was responsible for the development of their small drives range. After that I was part of a start-up enterprise designing and manufacturing small electrical equipment including motor controls. In the 1980s I joined Electro-Craft, one of the pioneers of brushless servomotor, providing application support for the European Sales activity. This involved evaluating proposed machine designs and recommending the correct servo components, including position controllers. It also involved on-site trouble-shooting. For a while I was also responsible for the design of some of the controllers and a range of small drives. Electro-Craft was eventually absorbed into Rockwell Automation, and I took over responsibility for the application support of Allen-Bradley Motion Control Products in Europe.



During this period I helped develop the Systex sizing tool used by Electro-Craft globally, and was later involved with the other authors in creating Motion Analyzer, the world class sizing and selection tool for Allen-Bradley
