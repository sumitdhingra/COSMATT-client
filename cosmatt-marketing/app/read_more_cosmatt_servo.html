ABOUT THE COURSE

COURSE DESCRIPTION

A servo system is usually defined as a system whereby the motion and position of a mechanism is controlled using a feedback device, by which the system can detect errors and act to correct them. The corrective action can be performed using electric motors or using pneumatic or hydraulic cylinders.

COSMATT is a course of instruction about electric servo systems and the optimal choice of servo motors and drives (amplifiers) to achieve specified requirements. Nevertheless, much of the instructive content applies to all servo systems irrespective of the type. It is designed to help both those with little knowledge of the subject to become conversant with it and those with existing knowledge to appreciate the finer points of the subject. The content is modularized and cover areas ranging from fundamentals to sophisticated applications. This allows users to skip subjects they are already comfortable with. The course content includes specially designed embedded calculation tools, which assist the user throughout the training process. This becomes extremely useful as it is impractical to manually calculate all possible permutations of a proposed system.

COURSE LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By the end of the course you will be able to:

  • Gain adequate knowledge of the fundamentals of dynamic motion and mechanical and electrical properties
  • Building Basic motion profiles in response to move requirements
  • Understanding differences between the treatment of linear and rotary motion
  • Understanding effects of changing the shape of the profile
  • Understanding how to evaluate a rotary load, incorporating inertia and including friction losses
  • Applying a motion profile to calculate the torque and speed requirements
  • Understanding how peak and continuous operating points are displayed in tabular and graphical forms
  • Compare the torque and speed required by the rotary load with the capabilities of various motors
  • Understand impact of adding coupling, motor inertia and losses
  • Plot the torque-speed curve of the motor with the operating points of the load
  • Be able to select an appropriate drive for the motor
  • Understanding the impact of transmission ratio on the choice of motor
  • Get to know the commonly available types of mechanical transmission systems
  • Learn how to deal with the added inertia and losses
  • Analyzing simple linear applications, including linear motion profiles and load configurations
  • To get an understanding of screw-type, and tangential (belt, chain or rack and pinion) mechanical systems and the impact on the transmission ratio

For a while I was also responsible for the design of some of the controllers and a range of small drives. Electro-Craft was eventually absorbed into Rockwell Automation, and I took over responsibility for the application support of Allen-Bradley Motion Control Products in Europe.



During this period I helped develop the Systex sizing tool used by Electro-Craft globally, and was later involved with the other authors in creating Motion Analyzer, the world class sizing and selection tool for Allen-Bradley
